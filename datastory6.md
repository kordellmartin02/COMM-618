This piece is similar to the New York Times’ data article on the correlation between presidential approval ratings and gas prices. Instead, the Times is evaluating how President Trump’s specific words, Truth Social posts, and memos immediately affect oil market prices. The article comes off as trustworthy, as it sources its data directly beneath its graphs and shows graphs that may conflict with each other to show a complete narrative. The graphs presented in the article were effective in showing the immediate effect of Trump’s words and how later prices were decided on with previous knowledge of his actions. I would have liked it if they expanded to see how oil markets reworked prices for Trump in his first administration, since gas prices were moderate then.
