JD Vance says crypto can help everyday Americans. Here’s how many actually use it.

NBC News used data that details cryptocurrency ownership among the U.S. population and connected it to comments from the White House praising crypto. Since Vice President JD Vance made a statement praising crypto for potentially helping Americans financially in the future. The data was easy to track since the article attributes and links to many surveys/studies. Most of the data came from the Pew Research Center and the U.S. Federal Reserve. The data was presented visually and in word, usually as attributes. 

The data was presented clearly, so it was easy to read and thought-provoking. Cryptocurrency ownership in the U.S. was divided by age, income, race, and gender, which potentially could lead to other stories. Nothing was confusing in the story. I would just have preferred the reporters to present the data with some explanation or discussion about why some groups in the U.S. population are purchasing more cryptocurrency than others.  I liked how they connected cryptocurrency trends in the U.S. to how crypto is treated in politics, often favored by the Trump administration through private events and purchases.

I would cover why younger men are buying cryptocurrency and if that has ties to its promotion by the current administration. How are they purchasing cryptocurrency, and what do they plan to do with it? I’m still wondering about what Americans use Bitcoin for. The title mentions the topic, but mainly sticks to ownership. The reporters could have gone in-depth about what Bitcoin and cryptocurrency are and what the White House is gaining from promoting the currency. They have one paragraph talking about how the president invited guests who purchased his personal meme coin. Including information that documents what the president and vice president have gained from praising cryptocurrency would have definitely helped push the White House more into the story.
