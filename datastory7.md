This is an interesting story that purposely focuses on past data because ICE has been withholding information because of the partial shutdown. The data is graphed in a manner that immediately stops at February 12, the last day the enforcement agency stopped sharing data. The data is sourced to ICE under the graphs and is critical of current detention rates as they rose sharply in the second Trump administration, especially before February 2026. I like how the reporters not only stuck with the topic of ICE withholding data, but they also found another angle on the rise of arrests of people lacking criminal records. The story could have expanded more on the locations that saw a quick jump in detentions in January, like Minnesota, but I don’t think it would have greatly impacted the story.
