Data from California shows that traffic deaths and injuries in Orange County have decreased since 2005. Though it is a steady decline, it contrasts with the numbers of casualties that remained stable from 2002 to 2005. Some questions that may arise from this chart are: “Why were there so many car deaths and injuries in Orange County, California, in the early 2000s, and why did numbers fall after 2005? Injuries sustained in traffic collisions in California seemed to peak in 2005. What changed?

While investigating reasons why deaths and serious injuries from Orange County traffic collisions had decreased, it was found that the state implemented a highway safety plan aimed at reducing traffic fatalities. The California Strategic Safety Plan may have been a factor in decreasing traffic collisions and deaths. If data on traffic collisions from 2010-2025 is collected, Americans could possibly see the 20-year impact of the California Strategic Safety Plan. The plan, while legally changing road rules, also changed the California driving culture. The plan addresses accountability when collisions happen, replacing the word “accident” with “crash.” A simple change of word could have had a profound effect on road safety in the state,
