This article had some data that surprised me. Although the state of healthcare in rural areas has been reported on constantly, putting a human face on the issue and data that support the overall topic really tied this piece together and made it unique. The data collected was sourced immediately after being presented in graphs. The graphs were interesting as well, since you don’t see many like them. I do like how the reporters measured different variables like age, health issues, and economic factors when comparing medical resource sin rurual areas. The use of color in one graph worked well since it gave some gravity to the story, as some states have high risks of hospital closures. Other graphs were interactive and changed based on the economic mobility of metropolitan and rural areas; these areas were contrasted to highlight the difference in accessibility. The graphs worked well for their narrative.
