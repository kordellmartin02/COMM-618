Olympic sports becoming even more difficult is a concept I haven’t thought of yet. The article does come with the data to prove it, showing how the frequency of difficult jumps has greatly increased due to modern pressures from judges. I would have preferred a human element in the article, if possible. The piece could have been expanded to include the effect of pressure in the sport. How do skaters react to this pressure, and does that itself increase the difficulty of the sport? The writers mention that the data on the total jumps of women Olympic skaters is stark since they often have fewer jumps, the average being lower than that of men’s Olympic skaters. They never got into why that is. They mention that women make the sport harder, but don’t say why. That would have made the piece even more interesting than it already is, since there appears to be a shift in expectation based on gender. When looking at the graphs, they can be intimidating, but the legend makes the context decipherable. Though they can still appear a bit jumbled for some readers. Also, the article wasn’t quite clear on where the data came from. It states that the writers had counted jumps, so I must assume they collected the data while watching the performances.
