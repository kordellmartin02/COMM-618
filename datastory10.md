I thought this piece was interesting since most of what’s reported is how much countries are paying the price for the conflict in the Strait of Hormuz, as opposed to this story that tells us how one country is withstanding it. I like how the piece contrasts China’s economic adaptation to varying oil market prices with its Asian neighbors that also consume lots of oil. The graphs contrasting this, however, are a bit confusing just because of the lack of specific numbers and data that sometimes fall off in the end. I do wish the journalists had expanded on how China’s neighbors, like Japan, India, and South Korea, may be affected by China’s stronghold against changing market prices. I left the article wondering if other countries are looking at China to implement policies like theirs or if the policies have direct effects on those countries.
