This data article does a good job portraying just how much money Elon Musk is expected to earn in the next year. Though comparing Musk’s possible income to the total salaries of every worker in select fields, such as cashiers and elementary school teachers, is helpful to show the magnitude of his wealth, I would have liked it if they had included some examples of salaries or at least the lowest and highest possible salaries of those workers. It would have been great to contrast and easier to grasp just how much the average person makes when put side-by-side to Elon Musk, one of the richest people in the world. Without this supplementary data for average salaries, the piece doesn’t quite feel balanced in my opinion. It feels empty. While it is great that they managed to pull this data, compile it, and contrast it to Musk’s billions, it doesn’t serve justice to those workers. Like when you search for counselors in the piece’s occupation tracker, it tells you that they earn less than $36 billion less than Elon Musk, but it doesn’t show any supporting data that could create more questions. If the data were there, readers could ask why the salaries of selected occupations are low and why they seem less important than Elon Musk’s position. In general, the story could have been stretched out more, but I don’t know how much time and brainpower that would include.
